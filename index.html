City of Arts and sciences

The City of Arts and Sciences (Ciudad de las Artes y las Ciencias) in Valencia, Spain, is a futuristic architectural complex dedicated to science, technology, nature, and art. Designed by Santiago Calatrava and Félix Candela, it features striking modern structures such as the Hemisfèric (IMAX cinema and planetarium), the Príncipe Felipe Science Museum, the Oceanogràfic (Europe’s largest aquarium), and the Palau de les Arts Reina Sofía (opera house). Surrounded by gardens and water features, it is one of Valencia’s most iconic attractions, blending education and entertainment in an innovative way.

Valencia is known for its beautiful palm trees, which line the streets, parks, and beaches, adding a Mediterranean charm to the city. These trees thrive in Valencia’s warm climate, where sunny days are abundant throughout the year. With over 300 days of sunshine annually, the city enjoys mild winters and hot summers, making it a perfect destination for outdoor activities and relaxation under the shade of swaying palms.
